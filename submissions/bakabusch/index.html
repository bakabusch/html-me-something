How to make the perfect pommodoro

An elegant meal that's easy to make

  • 1/2 cup high quality olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic sliced razor thin
  • 2 pints of ripe cherry tomatoes chopped in quarters
  • 2 tsp Balsamic Vinegar
  • 2 TB tomato paste
  • 1 pound dry vermicelli
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 10 leaves fresh basil chiffonade
  • 1/4 cup shreded asiago cheese
  • Freshly milled black pepper
  • Pinch of Kosher salt

Cook vermicelli until 'al dente' drain and rinse throughly with cold water (if you don't know how to cook pasta properly click here). Toss with 1/4 cup olive oil and set aside.

Heat a heavy saute pan on a medium-high flame. When pan is hot, add 1/2 cup olive oil; wait 10 seconds and add garlic. Stir quickly until garlic starts to brown. DO NOT allow garlic to get too dark or you'll need to start over. When garlic is ready, quickly add tomato paste and tomatoes. Reduce heat to a medium-low flame and stir until tomatoes start to break down. Add Balsamic vinegar, half the basil, and pasta; toss quickly. Plate the pasta and garnish with remaining basil and asiago cheese.

Mangia!
